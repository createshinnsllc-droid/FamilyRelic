## What I Need You to Know

> "Every gap, every absence — every last one of them — you were always on my mind. And that drove me to try to get through the hard times.
>
> You can't help nobody in life if you can't help yourself. My circumstances put my back against the wall so much that survival took precedence over me wanting to be emotionally or even physically there as much as I wanted to be.
>
> Sometimes two parents have to be on the same page to really not let the kids be affected — but a lot of times none of us is mature enough at the time to understand what we're doing. There's no pamphlet or instruction manual telling you how to be a good father. Especially if you never had one. Especially if you've never even seen one outside of the Cosby Show — or the ones you did see were so dysfunctional you were glad you didn't have one.
>
> A lot of men's stories don't get told. We never get to say how we really feel about our kids — because that's what gives us our purpose. For most men it's everything to us. But if you let uncontrollable circumstances — like not having the power to see or make pertinent decisions in your kids' life because of who you decided to sleep with — many of us are suffering in silence.
>
> I don't think I ever cried this much in one day over something I can't even explain. I don't know if it's joy. I don't know if it's the sadness of the realization. I just feel like today was the breakthrough I needed."
